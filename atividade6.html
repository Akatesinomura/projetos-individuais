Pirates!

How old pirate ships and crew used to operate, overly-simplified

Why would people join the pirates?

Although a life of crime sounds very risky, if you compare to the standard work conditions of the era, it wasnt so bad. A lot of people lived uneducated lives, capable only of menial work that paid poorly for many hours, or you could join a ship from the crown, where you would be paid poorly and be disrespected every turn by higher ranking officials. So the offer to participate in a crew with little to no hierarchy and be able to directly claim part of the loot from a plunder instead of being paid a low wage is indeed a sweeter deal.

Living in the ship

Well, the deal must have a catch, right? And it does! Living in a pirate ship was one of the worst experiences you could have in your life. Ships would often me overcrowded, to maximize manpower, and everyone would often be sick, due to disease spreading witihin the ranks, food was also terrible: shoe-sole consistency beef jerky, hardtack with bugs, fruits only if you had the luck to steal them from another ship.

Who was the crew?

The Crew consisted of 3 types of people:

  • Crewmembers: used for combat and basic ship keeping tasks (this included the ship "medic")
  • Quartermaster: handled the ship's economy, hiring, loot spliting and repairs
  • Captain: had to keep the crew's morale up, was the battle tactician and leader and was the one who did the "branding" of the pirates, to intimidate merchant ships
